Neuromechatronics Lab

Carnegie Mellon University's workspace for neuroscientists and engineers

About

The NeuroMechatronics Lab at Carnegie Mellon University, led by professor Doug Weber, is a multidisciplinary workspace for neuroscientists and engineers. Starting May of 2023, I worked with PhD students in the lab to construct multiple stimulator devices, as well as conduct validation testing. In July 2024, I designed and manufactured a rig to measure the torque on the wrist of a patient's hand. This data would be used in conjunction with EMG (electromyography) sensors to better understand the relationship between a patient's nerve cells and their muscle force output.

Spinal Column Stimulator

A spinal column stimulator controller, designed to work with a DS8R current stimulator. The stimulator attaches to the probe itself, which makes contact with certain regions on the spinal column. I assisted with the custom MATLAB software controlling the Arduino, as well as circuit analysis and troubleshooting the PCB. I also designed and 3D printed the housing for the electrical components.

Push button stim & VR Motion capture

A simpler, push-button 5V pulse stimulator. I was solely responsible for the design of the hardware and software. For the muscle motor function experiments, I designed two modes: single pulse and continuous output. A controller PCB for linking motion capture & data collection software to a virtual reality rig. This enclosure needed to be easily taken apart to change internal wiring.

Wrist Rig

This data would be used in conjunction with EMG (electromyography) sensors to better understand the relationship between a patient's nerve cells and their muscle force output. The body of the rig was assembled from aluminum 8020 stock and water-jetted aluminum plates. The forearm / wrist supports were 3D-printed PLA, prototyped for ergonomics. The system was designed around a FUTEK Loadcell.
